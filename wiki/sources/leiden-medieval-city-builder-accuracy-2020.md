## Raw Concept

Leiden Medievalists Blog essay on why medieval city-builders (Settlers, Knights and Merchants, Anno, Foundation, etc.) diverge from settlement archaeology.

## Narrative

### Main thesis [CONFIRMED]

Commercial medieval builders optimize **linear growth fantasy** — historically, villages were stable for centuries at subsistence with tithes, feudal extraction, and catastrophe risk.

### Historical realities rarely modeled

- Planned settlement layouts (street villages, circular manors) vs organic sprawl from town center
- Tithes, rents, feudal labor obligations
- Crop rotation, common pasture, seasonal flooding
- Raiding armies, epidemics, internal feuds

### Gameplay reasons for inaccuracy [CONFIRMED author]

- Linear growth = progress dopamine
- Harsh failure demoralizes casual players
- **Grid roads simplify pathfinding** — off-grid (Cities: Skylines) is engineering-heavy
- Marketing expects windmills, stone castles, busy peasants — not land surveying minigames

### Banished exception

High mortality / weather — growth not guaranteed — closer to subsistence struggle.

### Hobby takeaway

Stronghold-style **castle fantasy** is already ahistorical romantism; don't add accuracy burden in Tier 1. Optional Tier 2+: tithe events, bad harvest shocks.
