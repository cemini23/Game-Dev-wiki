## Raw Concept

Fan Q&A with **Simon Bradbury** (Firefly co-founder, Stronghold designer) during Stronghold 3 development.

## Narrative

### Design philosophy [CONFIRMED — interview text]

- **Return to SH1 basics** after SH2 overreach — fan feedback drove simplification
- **Map size** — large maps tested but rejected; extra march time without more combat = bad pacing
- **Troop count** — "more is often less"; SH3 fewer types than SH2 for balance
- **Crime system (SH2) out** — good/bad buildings from SH1 back; hovel strength scales with proximity to Keep
- **Engine** — Trinigy Vision chosen for physics, lighting, multi-core; custom engine cost too much dev time [CONFIRMED]
- **Day/night** — event-driven, not continuous sim clock; dusk→dawn mission beats
- **Modding** — editor ease prioritized; community maps showcased in-game (goal)
- **Fan suggestions** — small refinements adopted (e.g. sappers for wall assault); big swings rejected to preserve identity

### Quotes worth preserving

> "We pay a lot of attention to fan feedback and our return to a simpler game than 2 bears testament to that."

> "We have experimented with very large maps — but have found that this really only adds an extra 5 minutes while you wait for your troops to get into combat."

### Hobby project implications

- **Tier 1:** small map, fast unit response — Bradbury validates tight maps for pacing
- **Defer:** SH2 crime/honour complexity until Tier 2+
- **Steal:** event-driven time phases (optional night raid) vs full day/night sim
