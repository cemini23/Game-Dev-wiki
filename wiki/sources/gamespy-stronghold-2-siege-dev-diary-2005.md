## Raw Concept

Simon Bradbury **developer diary** on SH2 siege fantasy vs history — attack pipeline, new equipment, defensive tricks.

## Narrative

### Design intent [CONFIRMED]

Hollywood siege (breaches, ladder fights, keep showdown) over starvation/disease sim — but economic **starve-out** remains valid: sit outside and bleed castle dry.

### Attack pipeline [CONFIRMED]

1. Build **siege camp** (must protect from sallies)
2. Assess defenses → order counter-equipment
3. Soften walls with engines
4. **Dummy attack** — archers + **mantlets**
5. **Peasants fill moat** at weak spot (protected by **cat**)
6. Scale walls / breach / tower-by-tower advance

### SH1 vs SH2 wall combat [CONFIRMED]

Still can **catapult holes**, but defenders' **engineers refill breaches**. More often: **fight on walls** tower-by-tower vs single wall-collapse rush.

### New attack tools [CONFIRMED]

| Equipment | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **Cat** | Cover approach to walls |
| **Burning cart** | Burn traps/troops en route |
| **Tall siege towers** | Archers on portable high ground |
| **Laddermen** | Resupply from siege camp |

### New defenses [CONFIRMED]

- **Arrow slits** — defending archer near-invulnerable
- **Rock tipper** — crushes troops below
- **Rolling logs** — devastation on approach paths
- Moats, tunnels, hoardings, man traps, dogs (teased)

### castle-sim Phase D checklist

- Siege camp as production building
- Engineer repair loop on breaches
- Mantlet as mobile cover entity
- Moat fill labor (expendable peasants)

## Snippets

> "You could actually just sit outside with a large army and bleed a castle dry economically, but it's far more fun to go for the jugular."
