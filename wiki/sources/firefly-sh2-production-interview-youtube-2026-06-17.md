### Paul Harris — SH2 vs Legends [CONFIRMED — transcript]

- SH2 was Harris's **first Firefly ship title** as producer
- **Legends** reused SH2 economy "nuts and bolts" — shorter cycle, fantasy units
- Legends team **passion improvements** — UI scales to 4K (unrequested polish)

### Simon Bradbury — design philosophy [CONFIRMED]

- SH1 was **blank canvas** — "throwing cows, torture devices, burning people" all fair game
- Later titles **evolve/polish** but never same creative freedom
- Future wish: **more simulation bandwidth** — peasants with boils, daily castle life visible
- Research trips: real castle visits, reconstructed medieval villages inform layout

### Steam Edition UX [OBSERVED-YT — baK1Kiib7gY]

- **Right-click** build system added in Steam Edition (legacy was click-click)
- **Keep interior visible** — cited as satisfying detail
- **Gong → disease** can end MP sessions (comedy failure mode)

### castle-sim implications

- Bradbury's "simulation depth" aligns with operator clone — but **scope gate** one system at a time
- Visual peasant life = long-term polish, not M0.3D blocker
- Right-click + interior camera = UX reference for Godot input layer

## Snippets

Bradbury (paraphrased from transcript): initial Stronghold had freedom to put any idea on blank paper; sequels polish gameplay but rarely match that canvas.

Harris: Legends built on SH2 systems — economy tweak not rewrite — explains why SH2 Heaven mechanics transfer to Legends with edits.
