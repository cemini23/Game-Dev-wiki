## Raw Concept

Jason Ocampo **GameSpot** preview on honour economy, estates, and 3D siege — confirms victory condition change and dual campaigns.

## Narrative

### Campaigns [CONFIRMED]

| Mode | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **Path of War** | Classic defend/siege loop |
| **Path of Peace** | Economy-first storyline; limited beasts/bandits |
| **Free build** | Sandbox, no pressure |

### Honour loop [CONFIRMED]

Earn by **"living like a lord"** — luxury goods, church attendance, feasts, library accumulation, **mission objectives** (e.g. rescue warrior monks from wolves).

Spend on:

- Military units (knights etc.)
- **Village estates** on map (resources + defense obligation)
- **Rank promotions** (Freeman → … → Duke/King tiers)

**Critical rule:** promotion unlocks new buildables/units but **does not upgrade existing troops** — no mass obsolete→elite transform.

### Victory condition change [CONFIRMED]

To capture castle: fight to **top of main tower and hold until flag changes** — **defending lord may escape**; slaying lord not required.

### Siege & terrain [CONFIRMED]

- Catapults/trebuchets **crumble walls** (dust cloud breach)
- **Height advantage** for archers on hills
- Up to **8-player** multiplayer

### castle-sim note

Flag-hold victory is a **design alternative** to lord-kill — castle-sim can defer; honour-gated rank without unit transform is **must-match** for Kingmaker feel.

## Snippets

> "Honour is useless without popularity" — reinforced in companion Stronghold Nation articles; popularity gate still applies when spending honour.
